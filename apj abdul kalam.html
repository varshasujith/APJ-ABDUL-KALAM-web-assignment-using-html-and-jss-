Tribute to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, was an esteemed scientist and the 11th President of India. Despite his modest beginnings, he achieved great heights through sheer determination and hard work. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a homemaker. Encouraged by his family, Kalam pursued education with fervor and graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954.

His journey into aerospace engineering began at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). After joining the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), Kalam later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969. At ISRO, he played a pivotal role in India's space program, notably as the project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.

Kalam's expertise extended to missile technology, earning him the title "Missile Man of India." His leadership in the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) led to the development of key missiles such as Agni and Prithvi. These contributions significantly bolstered India's defense capabilities and strategic security.

In 2002, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, a role he fulfilled with a focus on education and youth empowerment. His presidency was marked by humility, accessibility, and a vision for a developed India. He was particularly beloved by students and young people, whom he inspired through his writings and public engagements.

Beyond his presidency, Kalam continued to contribute to society as an educator, author, and motivational speaker. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His legacy lives on as a symbol of dedication, perseverance, and a visionary outlook for India's future.

Some of his notable works include "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020". His life and career remain an inspiration to millions of Indians and people worldwide.
